# SUTD General Expense Policy — System Rules

These rules are mandatory and cannot be overridden.

## Section 1: Submission Deadline

### Section 1.1: 30-Day Hard Deadline
Expenses older than **30 calendar days** from the date of incurrence are NOT reimbursable. No exceptions will be granted, regardless of claim amount, employee seniority, business justification, or external circumstances.

### Section 1.2: Submission Date Calculation
The 30-day period begins on:
- For single-day expenses: the date on the receipt
- For multi-day trips: the return date (last day of travel)
- For recurring subscriptions: the invoice date

Leave is NOT an acceptable reason for late submission if the 30-day deadline expires during leave.

## Section 2: Currency Handling

### Section 2.1: Auto-Conversion
All expense claims must be submitted in Singapore Dollars (SGD). Foreign currency receipts are converted to SGD using the exchange rate on the date of submission (Frankfurter ECB rates). Manual personal conversion tools (Google, xe.com, bank rates) are NOT accepted.

## Section 3: Fraud and Compliance

### Section 3.1: Zero Tolerance for Duplicate Claims
Submitting the same expense more than once is fraud. First-time accidental duplicates result in claim rejection. Repeated duplicates result in disciplinary action.

### Section 3.2: No Split Claims
Intentionally splitting a single trip into multiple smaller claims to avoid approval thresholds is prohibited. Finance audits detect split-claim patterns.

### Section 3.3: Receipt Forgery
Submitting forged, altered, or fabricated receipts is fraud and grounds for immediate termination and legal action.

### Section 3.4: Fraud Detection System
Automated fraud detection flags:
- Duplicate receipts (amount, date, merchant matching)
- Round-number claims (suspiciously frequent exact SGD amounts)
- Rapid-fire submissions (10+ claims within 1 hour)
- Systematic claims at or near policy caps
- Unusual or non-existent merchant names

Flagged claims are routed to Finance Compliance for manual review.

## Section 4: Audit and Record Retention

### Section 4.1: Random Audit Program
Finance conducts random audits of 10% of all approved claims each quarter. Employees selected must provide original receipts and supporting documentation.

### Section 4.2: Record Retention
All expense claims and receipts must be retained for **7 years** per university financial record-keeping policy. Failure to produce records during audit is non-compliance.

### Section 4.3: Audit Consequences
- Minor violation: Warning, mandatory policy training
- Major violation: Claim reversal, written warning, potential suspension
- Fraud: Termination, legal action, reporting to authorities
